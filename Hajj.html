Introduction to Hajj

Hajj is the annual Islamic pilgrimage to Mecca, Saudi Arabia, and is a mandatory religious duty for Muslims that must be carried out at least once in their lifetime by all adult Muslims who are physically and financially capable.

It is one of the Five Pillars of Islam and demonstrates the solidarity of the Muslim people, and their submission to Allah (God).

General Information

Hajj Timeline & Rituals

Hajj occurs from the 8th to the 12th day of Dhu al-Hijjah, the last month of the Islamic calendar. Muslims from around the world gather in Mecca to perform a series of rituals:

  • Tawaf: Circling the Kaaba seven times in a counter-clockwise direction.
  • Sa'i: Walking seven times between the hills of Safa and Marwah.
  • Wuquf: Standing in prayer on the plains of Arafat from noon to sunset.
  • Ramy al-Jamarat: Stoning the devil in Mina.
  • Eid al-Adha: The Festival of Sacrifice.

Requirements for Hajj

Essential Requirements

To perform Hajj, pilgrims must meet specific requirements:

  • Be a Muslim (Hajj is only for followers of Islam)
  • Be of sound mind and mature (children must be accompanied)
  • Be physically and financially capable
  • Have necessary travel permissions

Documentation

  • Valid Passport: Ensure passport has at least six months validity
  • Hajj Visa: Special visa for pilgrimage
  • Vaccination Certificates: Proof of required vaccinations
  • Mahram Documentation: For women under 45 traveling with male guardian

Health Requirements

Pilgrims must complete required vaccinations:

  • Meningitis (ACWY)
  • COVID-19 (with booster if required)
  • Seasonal influenza
  • Polio (if coming from endemic countries)

Hajj Procedures

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Apply for a Visa

    Pilgrims must apply for a Hajj visa through the Saudi consulate or authorized travel agents at least 3-4 months before Hajj season.

  2. Book Travel and Accommodation

    Arrange flights, accommodation, and transport through an authorized Hajj travel agency approved by Saudi authorities.

  3. Prepare Spiritually and Physically

    Learn the rituals, make necessary preparations, and enter the state of Ihram at designated Miqat points.

  4. Perform Hajj Rituals

    Complete the sequence of rituals: Ihram, Tawaf, Sa'i, Wuquf at Arafat, Muzdalifah overnight stay, Ramy al-Jamarat, sacrifice, hair cutting, and farewell Tawaf.
