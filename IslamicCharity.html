Charity in Islam: Zakat & Sadaqah

📖 Qur’an 2:261

In Islam, Sadaqah, Zakat, and general donation are all forms of charity — but they are different in rules, purpose, and timing. Below is a deep, clear explanation.

1️⃣ ZAKAT (Mandatory Charity)

📖 What is Zakat? Zakat is an obligatory charity for Muslims. It is one of the Five Pillars of Islam. It purifies wealth and helps the needy.

💰 How much? 2.5% of your savings · Once a year · if wealth reaches Nisab

📊 Nisab (minimum threshold): 87.48g gold / 612.36g silver (value‑based)

👥 Who can receive Zakat? Poor (Faqir), Needy (Miskeen), Zakat collectors, New Muslims, Slaves, Debtors, Fi sabilillah, Travelers in need. (Surah At-Tawbah 9:60)

❌ Not to: Parents, grandparents, children, spouse.

🌿 2️⃣ SADAQAH (Voluntary Charity)

Sadaqah is voluntary charity — not compulsory. It can be money, food, clothes, helping someone, even a smile 😊 The Prophet ﷺ said: “A smile is charity.”

🗓️ Anytime, any amount – especially Ramadan, Fridays, hardship.
🌊 Sadaqah Jariyah: ongoing charity: well, mosque, tree, knowledge.

🤝 3️⃣ General Donation (Non‑Zakat)

Donation can be Zakat‑based, Sadaqah‑based, or general humanitarian help: orphans, disaster relief, medical, education.

  • Purifies wealth

✔ Difference between Zakat, Fitrana & Kaffarah

  • Zakat: 2.5% annual wealth, obligatory, 8 categories.
  • Fitrana (Zakat al-Fitr): paid before Eid, approx 2.5kg food or value, compulsory on every Muslim.
  • Kaffarah: penalty for broken fast/oath; feed 60 poor or fast 60 days.
